Education

1. Create a Conducive Learning Environment by
  1. Developing a comprehensive school safety plan to ensure students feel safe and secure while learning.
  2. Investing in technology and infrastructure to support online and blended learning, to accommodate diverse learning styles and needs.
  3. Providing professional development opportunities for teachers to improve their teaching skills and techniques.
2. Building of Libraries
  1. Increase funding for library construction and renovation, to ensure that all students have access to modern, well-equipped libraries.
  2. Create a system for regular updating and expanding the library's collection, to keep up with the latest information and resources.
  3. Hire additional librarians and support staff to provide research assistance and other services to students.
3. Creation of Courses and other Certification
  1. Develop a plan to identify in-demand skills and industries, and design certificate courses to meet those needs.
  2. Partner with employers and industry experts to ensure the courses are relevant and valuable for students.
  3. Offer the courses online or in-person, in a flexible format to accommodate working learners.
  4. Provide financial assistance for low-income students to participate in the certificate program.

Youth and Women Empowerment

  1. Promote policies that encourage young people and women to participate in politics, including campaign finance reform and efforts to increase voter turnout among youth.
  2. Improve access to credit and financial services for young people and women, to help them start and grow businesses.
  3. Encourage mentorship and networking opportunities for young people and women to help them succeed in their careers.

Community Development

  1. Increase funding for community development programs, such as affordable housing, community gardens, and small business development.
  2. Invest in infrastructure and transportation projects that benefit low-income communities, such as expanding public transportation options and improving sidewalks and bike lanes.
  3. Promote policies that support small businesses and local economies, such as tax incentives and grants for small business development.
  4. Encourage citizen participation in community development through programs such as community meetings, workshops, and town halls.
